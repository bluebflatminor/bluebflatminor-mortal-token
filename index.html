The Mortal Token

A Coherence-Gated Persistence Filter for Photonic AI Acceleration

↓   Download v7 PDF ↗   GitHub Repository
Thesis

We propose the mortal token as a coherence-gated persistence filter with no absolute reference frame, implemented in grain boundary trap states of ferroelectric-graphene photonic MVM devices. It bounds persistence against step-change coherence violations. Whether it bounds persistence against slow coherent drift depends on Unknown Eleven and is not assumed.

The core argument

The Inversion

The modal token introduced in The Conversion Floor is implicitly immortal. Its coherent complex field state persists at full fidelity until external noise degrades it. The entire conversion floor framework is built around resisting that degradation.

Decay is not the enemy. Unverified persistence is.

The mortal token proposes temporal self-limitation as a fifth required property of the modal token: a binary-flag leaky integrator m(t) with programmable decay lifetime τ, instantiated in grain boundary trap states. A token that cannot be renewed by coherent signal is not permitted to persist.

m(t) = m₀ · exp(−t / τ)

The conversion floor from the parent paper extends to three axes:

b ≥ max( f(δₘ), g(σ_mod), h(m, τ) )

where h(m, τ) is the governance tax — the ADC precision cost of operating near the mortality boundary — formalized as an SNR-constrained scaling ansatz anchored to published HfO₂ noise data.

Scope

What the Filter Does and Does Not Bound

Bounds: persistence against step-change coherence violations.

Does not bound: persistence against slow coherent drift.

Does not verify: veracity. A coherently wrong token renews indefinitely.

A signal that moves the token's state by sub-threshold increments while maintaining trajectory consistency at each renewal step will not trigger failure. This is a structural property of path-dependent coherence without external anchoring — not an implementation flaw. Whether grain boundary quantization provides an endogenous brake is Unknown Eleven.

A well-characterized filter with a known hard boundary is more useful than an overclaimed primitive.

Open questions

Named Unknowns

Five-A
Raman proxy reliability Is the Raman perturbation from a renewal event a reliable proxy for γ, or do competing mechanisms — strain, phonon coupling, surface roughness — contribute at comparable magnitude? Prerequisite
Nine
Goldilocks Pareto frontier Does the G-versus-τ Pareto frontier contain workable operating points where filter utility and Goldilocks margin are simultaneously satisfied? Open
Nine-A
Latency-noise Pareto What is the Pareto frontier between readout integration time t_int and effective σ_read(t_int) for Route I below 100 ns? At fast timescales, σ_read may be shot-noise limited rather than trap-state limited. Open
Ten
Coherence test specification Is S(ψ, s) from Appendix A.3 the appropriate renewal test? A negative resolution may be cost-additive (alternative measure, same architecture) or class-changing (temporal asymmetry required, repositions toward STDP). Full class-change detection requires pulse-shaping hardware not in the primary protocol. Deferred phase
Ten-A
Functional form of γ(m) Monotonic weakening as m decays is a testable expectation, not a physical consequence. Non-monotonic behavior, hysteretic plateaus, and transient strengthening are alternative regimes. Open
Eleven
Minimum resolvable drift per renewal interval Does Route I exhibit Regime A (single-scale quantization — endogenous drift brake) or Regime B (distributed trap dynamics — no discrete brake, step-change filter only)? The answer determines whether physics closes the coherent drift vulnerability or leaves it open. Open — hardest test
